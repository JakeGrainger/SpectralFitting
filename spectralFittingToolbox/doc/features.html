Features

Spectral Fitting Toolbox includes a variety of different features, including model fitting, simulation and non-parametric spectral density estimation.


Model Fitting

The main purpose of Spectral Fitting Toolbox is to enable the user to fit models for the spectral density function of a continuous time stochastic process to observed data. Spectral Fitting Toolbox comes with a variety of different techniques for achieving this. These range from curve fitting techniques such as least squares, to quasi-likelihoods such as the De-biased Whittle likelihood.

Spectral Fitting Toolbox requires the user to provide a parametric form for the spectral density function of the continuous time stochastic process. The user may also specifify the spectral density of the sampled, discrete time, process; or the autocovariance. However, Spectral Fitting Toolbox also has procedures to approximate these directly from the spectral density function of the continuous time process.


Simulation

As a secondary feature, Spectral Fitting Toolbox also allows the user to simulate Gaussian processes with a defined spectral density function. Simulated processes simulate the Gaussian process that would arise from sampling a continuous time Gausssian process, with a given spectral density function, at a sampling interval defined by the user.


Non-parametric spectral density estimation

Additionally, Spectral Fitting Toolbox also provides the user with classes to perform non-parametric estimation of the spectral density function. These classes include methods to easily visualise the obtained estimate.
